The Reality

Most retail software is built for businesses with dependable power, dependable internet, and budget for both.

That's not the reality for most stores.

Connectivity is inconsistent

Data bundles run out. Routers fail. Cloud-only tools go dark the moment the network does.

Power is not guaranteed

Systems that assume "always-on" infrastructure quietly fail the businesses that need them most.

Off-the-shelf tools don't fit

Built for markets with different assumptions, they add friction instead of removing it.

The result: shop owners fall back on notebooks and gut feeling, even after paying for a POS machine.

Our Approach

Trexave is building a Retail Operating System — infrastructure designed around the environment businesses actually operate in, not an idealized one.

Our approach starts with the store PC, not the cloud. The computer already sitting behind the counter becomes the system's home base — staff devices on the shop's own WiFi talk to it directly. No dependency on an internet connection just to record a sale.

We're building this in layers, starting with what a single store needs today, with room to grow toward multi-store and cloud-connected tools as businesses scale.
